# Demo Recording Script — 2 Minutes

**URL:** https://giwojno.pl/review-analyzer/
**Tip:** Record with OBS or screen recorder. Zoom browser to ~90% so everything fits. Move mouse slowly and deliberately.

---

## Scene 1: Dashboard (0:00–0:25)

**Action:** Open the website. Pause briefly on the dashboard.

**Voiceover:**
"Here's our live web app. The dashboard gives an instant overview — 28,332 reviews, 65 products, 6 categories. You can see the sentiment breakdown — 90% positive — and how reviews distribute across categories. Fire Tablets and Batteries dominate."

**What to show:**
- Stat cards at the top
- Sentiment bar
- Hover briefly over the charts

---

## Scene 2: Categories API (0:25–0:55)

**Action:** Click "Categories (API)" tab. Click on "Fire Tablets" card.

**Voiceover:**
"Let's look at what the AI wrote. These are the Claude-generated articles. I'll open Fire Tablets — our largest category. You get a full recommendation article: top products, common complaints, the worst-rated product, and a buying recommendation. This was generated from 14,000 classified reviews for 43 cents."

**What to show:**
- Category cards grid
- Click Fire Tablets
- Scroll slowly through the article
- Point out the structure: top picks, complaints, avoid section

---

## Scene 3: Categories Local (0:55–1:15)

**Action:** Click back. Click "Categories (Local)" tab. Click on "Fire Tablets" again.

**Voiceover:**
"Now the same category, but generated by the local Flan-T5 model running on GPU. Notice the difference — it's just a few sentences with product names and ratings. Same data, same task, but 250 million parameters versus an API model. Both are deployed here so you can compare directly."

**What to show:**
- Click back to grid
- Switch to Categories (Local) tab
- Click Fire Tablets
- Let the short output speak for itself

---

## Scene 4: Products (1:15–1:35)

**Action:** Click "Products" tab. Filter or scroll. Click a product with a summary.

**Voiceover:**
"The Products tab shows all 65 products. You can filter by category. Let me click on the Fire HD 8 — it has an AI-generated summary with a verdict, strengths, weaknesses, and who should buy it. Products with 20 or more reviews get these summaries."

**What to show:**
- Products table overview
- Use category filter dropdown
- Click a product (Fire HD 8 or similar)
- Show the summary popup

---

## Scene 5: Reviews (1:35–1:55)

**Action:** Click "Reviews" tab. Apply a filter.

**Voiceover:**
"Finally, the Reviews explorer. You can filter by category, sentiment, rating, or search for keywords. Let me filter for Negative reviews in Fire Tablets — these are the actual reviews our model classified, and they fed into the summarization. Everything connects."

**What to show:**
- Reviews tab overview
- Select "Fire Tablets" cluster filter
- Select "Negative" sentiment filter
- Show filtered results
- Maybe search for a keyword like "battery" or "slow"

---

## Closing (1:55–2:00)

**Voiceover:**
"All of this runs as a static site — no backend, no database. Just pre-computed JSON files on shared hosting."

---

## Recording Tips

1. **Browser:** Use Chrome, zoom to 90%, hide bookmarks bar
2. **Resolution:** Record at 1920x1080
3. **Mouse:** Move slowly, pause on each section
4. **Voiceover:** Record separately or narrate live — separate is cleaner
5. **Transitions:** Don't rush between tabs, let each view breathe for 2-3 seconds
6. **Fallback:** If live site is slow, have screenshots ready as backup
